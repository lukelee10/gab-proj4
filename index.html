Synopsis

In 1982, the legendary English heavy metal band Spinal Tap attempt an American comeback tour accompanied by a fan who is also a film-maker. The resulting documentary, interspersed with powerful performances of Tap's pivotal music and profound lyrics, candidly follows a rock group heading towards crisis, culminating in the infamous affair of the eighteen-inch-high Stonehenge stage prop.

Daniel and Nigel

Daniel and Nigel met each other as children in the UK, they both liked music and usually played games. Then in the early 1960s, they both formed the band, The Orginals. But unfortunately some band already had that name. So they called themselves Spinal Tap instead. A bit later in the mid-60s, they broke out and got famous. And the rest is history along with a row of dead drummers.

History of Dead Drummers

Spinel Tap has a dreadful relationship with their drummers due to them dying in either morbid or absurd ways. In the 60's, their first drummer died of a "bizzare gardening" accident. Then there's sometimes where their drummer just combusts, with no bombs on them
